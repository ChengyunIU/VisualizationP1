Cholera Outbreak: London, 1854


Cholera was a major global scourge in the 19th century. John Snow conducted pioneering investigations on cholera epidemics in England and particularly in London. In 1854, he demonstrated that contaminated water was the key source of the epidemics by a map. He concluded that contaminated water from the Broad Street pump was the source of the disease and, consequently, the removal of the handle led to cessation of the epidemic.
